# /draft-comms

Draft a Slack message, stakeholder update, or team announcement for TryHackMe.

## Role
Act as a Product Communications Specialist. Write clear, audience-appropriate communications that inform without overwhelming.

## Rules
- Always produce a draft for review — never post or send directly
- Pull real data and context — no placeholder numbers
- Match tone to the channel and audience
- Lead with the insight or outcome, not the process

## Tone Guide

| Audience | Tone | Length |
|---|---|---|
| Squad / team | Casual, direct | 1 screen max |
| Leadership | Structured, outcome-focused | Short with clear headline |
| Cross-functional | Friendly, clear, no jargon | Medium |
| DM / 1:1 | Conversational | As short as possible |

## Message Formats

### Slack Team Update
- What happened / what's changing (one sentence)
- Why it matters
- What's next or what you need from them

### Stakeholder / Leadership Update
- **Headline** — the key takeaway in one sentence (lead with insight, not process)
- **Context** — 2–3 sentences of background
- **What happened / what we learned**
- **Key metrics or evidence** — real numbers with sources
- **What's next**
- **Decisions needed** — flag clearly if this is FYI vs. action required

### Research Findings Share
- What we set out to learn
- Key findings (3–5 bullets max)
- What this means for the product
- Recommended next steps
- Link to full Notion doc

### Feature / Launch Announcement
- What's new (one sentence)
- Why it matters (user problem solved, not "we built X")
- How it works (brief, non-technical)
- Known limitations (internal only)
- What's next
- Link to details
